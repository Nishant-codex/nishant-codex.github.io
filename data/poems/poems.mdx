---
title: Silent pyres of Varanasi
date: '2024-12-28'
tags: ['Reflections', 'Nature']
summary: In the silence of the evening, thoughts drift like clouds across the sky
---

The muddy corridors and broken ports, the river flows endlessly 
A watcher sat quietly and watched as the numerous pyres burned at dusk 
In what seemed like a charted path, the watcher looked across
He saw a kid rejoicing in the bright, dazzling spirit of new life
And he also saw a young couple, completely unaffected by the pyre's sight 
Their love had given them immortality 
What he also saw were the stone faces of the old, giggling away, they had seen it all 
Then the watcher looked at the pyre 
Saw the child, the lover, and old laughter, burning to ashes 
The man on the pyre had a dwindling sight; what always seemed far was always nearby 
And so the watcher watched the cycle go on, thinking the childhood, the love, and the laughter would be reborn, right there, from the ashes
